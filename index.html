Introduction

This collection highlights my journey into the IT and networking world, showcasing my skills, experiences, and achievements so far. Inside, you'll find examples of my career projects, mini-labs, and hands-on exercises — including cloud management, virtualisation, and cybersecurity work.

These projects have helped me develop practical IT skills, problem-solving abilities, and an understanding of how systems work in real-world environments. Personal Profile

About Me

Growing up, I was always the person my household turned to for technical problems. Whether it was fixing devices, setting up WiFi, or troubleshooting issues, my family relied on me. Even solving minor problems made me feel proud and valuable.

Over time this naturally developed into an interest in IT, though I didn't fully realise it was my true passion at first. One day someone said, "You're good with technology — why haven't you made a career out of it?" That question changed everything.

I'm here today because I want to succeed more than ever. My dad never got to see me have a career, and I felt I wasn't fulfilling my potential. Now that he's gone, I feel even more driven. I wish I could have shown him what I could do — and now it feels like my duty, to him and to myself, to make this count.

I'm interested in tech because I enjoy problem-solving and structured thinking. I like understanding how systems work and finding logical solutions when things go wrong. Technology gives me focus, clarity, and the satisfaction of creating tangible results from knowledge and effort.

I'm also actively working on my articulation — reading more, practising tongue twisters, and structuring my thoughts more clearly. This matters for networking roles where explaining technical issues to colleagues and clients is just as important as solving them.

Where I'm Headed

I'm currently doing a Skills Bootcamp with The Development Manager in Tech and Cyber, covering hardware, networking, operating systems, and security — some of the most important and fast-growing areas in technology today.

Short-term: Get hands-on experience in IT support to build strong fundamentals. A solid IT support foundation improves future endeavours and provides a safe baseline — it's the natural starting point for anyone wanting to move into networking.

Long-term: Become a Network Engineer, working with networks and infrastructure to keep systems running smoothly. This bootcamp is giving me the knowledge and confidence to work steadily toward that goal.

Beyond formal training, I'm exploring the industry in practical ways — reaching out to employers for career advice, joining IT forums like Reddit to understand real-world problems, and connecting with professionals who can share their experience of getting into the IT field.
