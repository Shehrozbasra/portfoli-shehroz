✏️ Personal Profile

About me —

Growing up, I was always the person my household turned to for technical problems. Whether it was fixing devices, setting up WiFi, or troubleshooting small issues, my family relied on me. Even solving minor problems made me feel proud and valuable.

Over time, this naturally developed into an interest in IT, though I didn't fully realise it was my true passion at first. I went through different jobs, and one day someone said, "You're good with technology, why haven't you made a career out of it?"

That question made me reflect. I realised I had been following normal career paths instead of pursuing what I was naturally good at.

I'm here today in this skills bootcamp because I want to succeed more than ever. My dad never got to see me have a career, and personally, I felt useless without fulfilling my potential. Now that he's gone, I feel even more driven. I wish I could have shown him what I could do when he was here, and now it feels like my duty to him and to me to make this count.

I'm interested in tech because I enjoy problem-solving and structured thinking. I like understanding how systems work and finding logical solutions when things go wrong. Technology gives me focus, clarity, and the satisfaction of creating tangible results from knowledge and effort.

✏️ My Career Goal

My Career Goal

I'm currently doing a Skills Bootcamp with the Development Manager in Tech and Cyber. We're covering hardware, networking, operating systems, and security. I think these are some of the most important and fast-growing areas today; tech is everywhere now!

Right now, my short-term goal is to get some hands-on experience in IT support to build the basics. Long-term, I want to become a Network Engineer, working with networks and infrastructure to keep systems running smoothly. This bootcamp is helping me get the knowledge and confidence to work toward that goal.

✏️ My Career Progression

My Career Progression —

What I learned:

I spent time researching different tech roles, especially ones in networking, IT support, and data. I practised summarising job descriptions and spotting the skills employers look for, like problem-solving, logical thinking, and technical know-how. I also looked at companies to see what they value in their IT/Networking teams.

What I now understand better:

I can see that moving into networking takes both experience and learning the foundations first. Doing support tasks and small technical challenges now will make it easier to step up into network-focused roles later. It helped me get a clearer idea of what I want to do and how to plan for it.

✏️ Career and Experience

Career and Experience

A good way to still keep my job goal of becoming a network engineer is to see the lower level of the job — such as IT support. It's good to have this as the basis, as it will improve for future endeavours and help learn the basics to a further degree, but also provide a safe backup plan.

The experience I suggest is unique — going to as many employers and asking for career advice is a good way to understand how to actually reach that goal of joining the IT industry. There are also more ways, like joining IT forums such as Reddit. This can develop my understanding of what's happening, the common problems people face, and also connect with professionals.

Articulation

I'm currently working on improving my articulation by reading more and practising simple tongue twisters. Sometimes my explanations aren't as clear as I want them to be, so this is helping me speak more confidently and structure my thoughts better. Improving my communication is important, especially for the job roles I want in networking, where I'll need to explain technical issues clearly to colleagues or clients. It also helps me organise my ideas, focus on the key information, and think more clearly overall. This will benefit me in interviews, at work, and in daily life.

Virtual Network Simulation
Situation

During a virtual IT training course, I was asked to design a small office network and ensure devices could communicate securely.

Task

My task was to set up the network, configure VLANs, assign IP addresses, and implement basic security measures.
